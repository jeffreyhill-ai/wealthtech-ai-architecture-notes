# Why Advisor AI Needs Review Queues

Advisor-facing AI should not treat uncertainty as a copywriting problem.

When a system extracts a balance from a statement, notices a contradiction between two documents, or infers that a recurring deposit may be payroll, the product should not hide that uncertainty inside a confident paragraph. It should turn it into reviewable work.

A review queue is where AI becomes useful without pretending to be fully autonomous.

Good review queues make provisional facts visible. They show the source, confidence, extraction method, conflict, suggested action, and downstream impact. They let the advisor approve, correct, reject, or request more information before anything becomes part of the client record.

This changes the role of AI from "answer machine" to workflow accelerator:

- extract possible facts from messy evidence
- identify missing or contradictory information
- propose advisor questions
- draft narratives only from reviewed facts
- preserve an approval trail

The advisor remains responsible for judgment. The system becomes responsible for making the work faster, clearer, and easier to defend.
